1.The state motion and rest are relative . Explain ?

A. Rest and motion are relative because they depend on the observer's frame of reference. Hence on the basis of the reference frame of the observer, the state of motion or state of rest appear to us. Hence they are relative .

2. How to is average velocity different from instantaneous velocity ?

A. Average velocity : It is as the ration of total dispalcement to the total time taken . Instantaneous velocity : It is the velocity of the particle at any instant time .

3. Give an example where the velocity of an object is zero but its acceleration is not zero ?

A. In case of vertically projected body, the velocity at maximum height is zero, but its acceleration is g, which is not zero .

5. Can the velocity of an object be in a direction other then the direction of acceleration of the object ? If so, give an example .

A. Yes , In case of an object projected vertically upwards, the velocity is directed vertically up wards but acceleration due to gavity is directed vertically downwards .

6. A parachutist flying in an aeroplane jumps when it is at a height of 3km above ground. He opens his parachute when he is about 1km above ground. Describe his motion ?

A. A parachutist jumped from an aeroplane at a height of 3km above the ground . Now he has two dimentional motion . He possess uniform velocity equal to plane velocity in horizontal direction itll he opens parachute at a height 1km above the ground and he has vertical downward motion due to gravitational force . So he follows parabolic path till he opens parachute at a height 1km above the ground . After parachute is opended, we should consider ari resistance (viscous drag ), air buoyancy acting on him along with gravitational force. In presence of this forces the net acceleration will become zero after falling through certain height, he attains constant velocity called terminal velocity. Further he falls along a straight line downwards with the same terminal velocity till he reaches the ground .

Because the bird and the fruit have same velocity in horizontal direction b) The trajectory of the fruit as seen by a person on the ground is parabola.
